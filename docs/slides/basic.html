Chapter 1

Behold, the arsenal of thy artifacts, adventurer!

We are starting off together at the camp. You'll first acquaint yourself with the essential tools and basics, build your knowledge for your adventure with Django and learn everything to survive in the wilderness.

Our expedition shall delve into the vast realm of the Django Fullstack Framework. Fear not, for your current skill level holds no sway, as a suitable starting point awaits both novice and adept alike. To prepare for this grand endeavor, ensure you have PyCharm (Professional Edition), Python 3.11, and Poetry 1.5.1 already installed.

Some trouble shooting:
  • Check out "Diving into Django - Foundation" chapter 1 in eXXcellent Learning.
  • Check if the python path in correctly added the user environment.
  • If PyCharm tells you that Poetry is missing check this path %\AppData\Roaming\pypoetry\venv\Scripts.

Django arrives fully equipped with all the gear you need to swiftly and efficiently embark on your quest. A trusty companion that will accompany you every step of the way is 'django-admin.' You can summon it within your project by invoking: 

> python manage.py

In the realm of Django, logically related pieces of code are bundled into apps, akin to sacred artifacts. These artifacts should strive to be as reusable as possible, echoing the timeless mantra of 'Choose wisely, and let them be utilized across realms.'

Let us now create your inaugural artifact, known as the First App. We could venture forth manually, or we can entrust the task to the venerable 'django-admin' itself, for it possesses the power to make it so effortlessly: 

> python manage.py startapp first_app

Indeed, for Django to recognize and embrace your App, it must be registered within the sacred tome known as 'settings.py'.

Behold, the birth of your First App has come to pass! Now, we must embark on a perilous quest to infuse it with substance and meaning, for an artifact without content is but an empty relic awaiting its destiny.
